About Northwest AI

We make mid-market companies AI native.

Northwest AI embeds engineers and consultants into companies doing $10M to $500M, builds their AI agents, trains their people, and runs their AI department. Bootstrapped, profitable, and built on shipped work, not slideware.

What we believe

The technology was never the hard part.

MIT found that about 5% of enterprise AI pilots produce real P&L impact. The other 95% spend and stall. We've watched why, from inside hundreds of engagements: AI fails on adoption, not capability. People get generic answers from a tool that doesn't know their company, and they quit.

So we built the firm around the three things adoption actually takes: engineers who ship real builds, trainers who change daily behavior, and Company Brain, the context system that makes AI specific to your business from the first prompt. Most firms do one of these. Doing all three, every month, is the whole point.

And we work where the big firms don't: the independent mid-market. Construction, insurance, healthcare, manufacturing. The companies that run the real economy, where the gap between AI talk and AI work is widest, and where closing it pays off fastest.

The story

It started with a contract manager in construction.

The engineering came first. Asad Malik and Awais Tariq started a software development firm in 2018, building custom tools for companies. When OpenAI opened its first API, their clients started asking for internal AI tools, so they built them. By the time the world was debating what AI might do someday, their team had been shipping it inside real businesses for years.

The spark came later, from Wyatt's mom. She manages contracts for a construction company, and one day she was told she couldn't use AI. No training, no guidance, no legal help with the contracts crossing her desk every day. Just a ban.

Wyatt had been building custom tools with Asad and Awais, and the contrast was hard to ignore. Inside the dev shop's world, AI was already doing real work. Outside it, the people who needed it most were being locked out, because nobody would teach them what it could do, what it couldn't, and how to use it safely. The problem was never the tool. It was that nobody was educating the people.

So they bet the company on it. A hard pivot into AI: the 2018 engineering bench, plus consultants who teach in plain language, aimed at the companies everyone else ignores. That partnership became Northwest AI. The rest is history, and we've been building together ever since.
